About Mangga

Mangga is the Indonesian and Malay word for "mango," a tropical fruit that is native to South Asia. Mangoes are a popular food in many parts of the world, and are known for their sweet, juicy flesh and fragrant aroma. The skin of a mango can be green, yellow, red, or a combination of these colors, and the fruit can be oval or round in shape. Mangoes are a good source of vitamins A and C, as well as dietary fiber. They can be eaten fresh or used in cooking, and are often used in dishes such as chutneys, salads, and drinks.
